# Data Retention and Deletion Policy

**Control**: ISO/IEC 27002 A.8.10

## 1. Purpose
This policy defines the standard retention periods for key data categories within the MayWin Core Backend. The objective is to ensure data is retained for as long as it is needed for business, legal, or operational purposes, and securely deleted thereafter.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all data stored and processed by the MayWin Core Backend, including its PostgreSQL database and associated backups.

## 3. Retention Schedule
The following table outlines the retention periods for different categories of data.

| Data Category | Description | Retention Period | Justification |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **User Account Records** | User profile information stored in the `users` table, including email, name, and roles. | Active User: Indefinite.<br>Deactivated User: 3 years after deactivation. | Required for ongoing application access. Retained post-deactivation for audit history and potential reactivation. |
| **Scheduling Data** | Schedule containers, assignments, and job history. | 5 years from the end date of the schedule. | Business requirement for historical analysis, reporting, and dispute resolution. |
| **Worker Availability & Preferences** | Records of worker-submitted availability and preferences. | 2 years after the entry date. | Relevant for short-to-medium term scheduling and analysis, but becomes stale over time. |
| **Database Backups** | Full daily backups of the PostgreSQL database. | 30 days. | Aligns with the Recovery Point Objective (RPO) and provides a reasonable window for disaster recovery. See `BUSINESS_CONTINUITY_PLAN.md`. |
| **Security Audit Logs** | Logs generated by `SecurityLoggerInterceptor`, capturing API requests, user actions, and errors. | 1 year. | Required for security incident investigation, compliance audits, and fulfilling legal requests. See `LOG_MONITORING.md`. |
| **Authentication Logs** | A subset of security logs related to login attempts (`POST /auth/login`). | 1 year. | Critical for detecting and investigating brute-force attacks and unauthorized access attempts. |
| **Job Artifacts (S3)** | Normalized inputs, solver outputs, and KPIs stored in the AWS S3 bucket. | 5 years (aligned with Scheduling Data). | Artifacts are part of the historical record for a given schedule job. See `CLOUD_SECURITY.md`. |

## 4. Data Deletion
- **Automated Deletion**: Where feasible, automated scripts or database jobs will be implemented to enforce the retention periods defined above.
- **Manual Deletion**: Data may be deleted upon a verified request from a data subject in accordance with the `PRIVACY_POLICY.md` and legal obligations.
- **Secure Deletion**: Deletion means permanent removal from active application databases. Data may persist in backups until those backups expire as per this policy.
